So something (relatively) new and fun in browsers, are these two APIs: ReadableStream and WritableStream. ðŸ˜®

What they allow you to do, is basically send and receive chunks of things progressively (that then combine into the final large thing), as opposed to moving around entire things.

Below is a living example of streaming vs. conventional transfer:

I am a bunch of text who lives here, but I'm bored of living here so I want to move trololololololol.

Pretty cool, huh?

So basically, what's happening is this:

  • On load of this page, we construct a new ReadableStream
  • When we construct it, we say HEY, EVERY x SECONDS, TAKE A LETTER FROM THE LEFT COLUMN
  • And then we wait...
  • ...until someone clicks MOVE ME
  • When someone clicks this button, we _pipe_ the content of the ReadableStream into a WritableStream
  • When the WritableStream receives data, we tell it to remove the character from the left column, and add it to the right one.

All of this is visible in the source code (index.js). There's more cool things in the examples folder. Go, explore, have fun! Woooo!
